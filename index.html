Part 1 · Weather-derivative pricing

Boreas · Weather Derivatives

I model daily temperature for the US cities on the CME weather market with an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process and machine-learning forecasters (Ridge, Random Forest, XGBoost), trained by nested cross-validation on seventy years of data.

The models then price Heating Degree Day call options, backtested over five winters (2020 to 2025). Boreas is the end-to-end dashboard that turns these models into live prices for commodity traders.

  • Ornstein–Uhlenbeck
  • Ridge / RF / XGBoost
  • HDD options
  • Monte-Carlo
  • Streamlit

Part 2 · Extension

From Winter Temperatures to Natural-Gas Volatility

A follow-up study that links winter temperature anomalies to the volatility of Henry Hub natural gas. I fit a Schwartz–Smith two-factor state-space model with a Kalman filter, then a weather-coupled Student-t GARCH that feeds temperature anomalies into the volatility dynamics.

The honest takeaway: the temperature signal is genuine on historical data, but the edge largely evaporates on new data. Skill measured on history rarely survives on live markets.

  • Schwartz–Smith
  • Kalman filter
  • GARCH
  • Henry Hub
