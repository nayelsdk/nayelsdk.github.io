Part 1 · Weather-derivative pricing

Temperature Models for Weather Derivatives & Commodity Trading

I model daily temperature for the US cities of the CME weather market, combining an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck mean-reverting process with machine-learning forecasters (Ridge, Random Forest, XGBoost), trained by nested cross-validation on seventy years of data.

These models then price Heating and Cooling Degree Day (HDD/CDD) options, by Monte-Carlo simulation and closed-form formulas, back-tested over five winters from 2020 to 2025.

Boreas ties it together: an end-to-end interactive dashboard that turns the models into live prices for commodity traders.

  • Ornstein–Uhlenbeck
  • Ridge / RF / XGBoost
  • HDD/CDD options
  • Monte-Carlo
  • Financial Mathematics

Part 2 · Extension

Winter Temperatures as a Driver of Natural-Gas Volatility

I extract the short- and long-term factors of Henry Hub natural gas with a Schwartz–Smith two-factor state-space model, fitted by a Kalman filter.

A weather-coupled GARCH(1,1) then feeds winter temperature anomalies into the volatility dynamics. The temperature signal is genuine on historical data, but the edge largely evaporates out of sample. Skill measured on history rarely survives on live markets.

  • Schwartz–Smith
  • Kalman filter
  • Volatility models
  • Weather-Coupled GARCH
  • Fat Tails distribution
  • NatGas spot & storage

Side project · 2025

Deep Learning Applied to Video Analytics

A deep-learning pipeline that detects and tracks people and vehicles across 36 fixed surveillance cameras, indoor and outdoor. I fine-tuned a YOLOv8 detector, chosen over Faster R-CNN for its speed and accuracy under limited GPU, and paired it with a lightweight IoU-matching tracker that keeps a persistent ID for each subject across frames.

Beyond raw accuracy, the focus is robustness: I stress-tested several fine-tuning strategies under adverse conditions such as rain, fog, darkness and harsh brightness, applied on the fly with Albumentations and evaluated on held-out cameras.
